## Why Traditional DEI Metrics Fail

Let's be honest: Most DEI metrics are theater.

**Problem 1: Representation Metrics Miss the Point**  
Knowing that 12% of your workforce identifies as LGBTQ+ tells you nothing about whether they feel safe, supported, or valued. It's a headcount, not a health check.

**Problem 2: Survey Data Is Biased and Delayed**  
Annual engagement surveys ask, "Do you feel included?" Six months later, you get aggregate results. By then, your top LGBTQ+ talent has already updated their LinkedIn and started interviewing elsewhere. Self-reported data from employees worried about retaliation? Not exactly reliable.

**Problem 3: Compliance Theater**  
You have a 47-page DEI policy. Congrats. Does anyone read it? Does it change behavior? Does it prevent harm? Policies on paper don't equal safety in practice.

**Problem 4: No Connection to Business Outcomes**  
Traditional metrics don't connect DEI to what the C-suite cares about: retention, productivity, innovation, and‚Äîmost importantly‚Äî**financial performance and risk reduction.**

The result? DEI teams drowning in data but starving for insights. Executives unable to justify budgets. Boards frustrated by vague "progress updates."

**Time for a new playbook.**
