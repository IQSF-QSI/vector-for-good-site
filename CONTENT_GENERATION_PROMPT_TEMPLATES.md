## ðŸ“± Template 2: LinkedIn Thought Leadership Post

### **Use Case:** Quick-hit insights, data stories, founder voice
### **Target:** Startup founders, HR leaders, investors, tech community
### **Frequency:** 2-3x per week

### **Prompt Structure:**

```
You are writing a LinkedIn post for Levi Hankins, Founder & CEO of Vector for Good.

TOPIC: [Your topic]
KEY MESSAGE: [Core insight or takeaway]
TARGET AUDIENCE: [Startup founders, HR leaders, investors, etc.]

POST GOALS:
- [Goal 1: e.g., "Drive demo bookings"]
- [Goal 2: e.g., "Build thought leadership on AI ethics"]
- [Goal 3: e.g., "Start conversation about ESG measurement"]

REQUIREMENTS:

1. **Hook (First 2 Lines):**
   - Must work standalone (visible before "see more" click)
   - Provocative question, surprising stat, or bold claim
   - Examples:
     * "I just watched a Fortune 500 company waste $3.2M because their AI couldn't handle pronouns."
     * "Hot take: Your DEI metrics are lying to you."
     * "We analyzed 10,000 LGBTQ+ safety incidents. Here's what nobody's talking about:"

2. **Story/Context (Lines 3-8):**
   - Personal anecdote, client story, or industry observation
   - Make it specific (names changed, but real details)
   - Build credibility and relatability

3. **Data/Proof (Lines 9-12):**
   - Specific statistic or research finding
   - Case study result
   - Industry benchmark
   - Must be verifiable and credible

4. **Insight/Takeaway (Lines 13-16):**
   - What does this mean?
   - Why should audience care?
   - What changes based on this info?

5. **CTA/Question (Last 2-3 Lines):**
   - Ask engaging question to drive comments
   - OR link to resource (blog post, demo, calculator)
   - OR call to action (DM me, comment below, share this)

6. **Hashtags (3-5 max):**
   - Mix of volume and niche
   - #ESG #DEI #LGBTQ+ #StartupLife #AI #SocialImpact #FutureOfWork
   - Research trending hashtags in your industry

7. **Formatting:**
   - Short paragraphs (2-3 lines max)
   - Use line breaks for readability
   - Strategic emoji use (1-2 max, never excessive)
   - Bold key phrases sparingly

VOICE GUIDELINES:
- Authentic founder voice (personal but professional)
- Confident without arrogance
- Vulnerable when it serves the story
- Data-driven but not robotic
- Occasionally contrarian or bold
- Always constructive
```
